Connecting people through creativity.

Flare is a digital platform designed to simplify and elevate the art commission experience. Built for both clients and creators, Flare provides a seamless, intuitive, and supportive environment where ideas can be matched with the right artistic talent. Whether users are looking for illustrators, animators, designers, or multidisciplinary creators, Flare makes it easy to discover artists, post project requests, and collaborate through real-time communication. Flare empowers creativity by connecting people with the tools, talent, and transparency they need to bring their visions to life.

Our mission is to create a thriving creative ecosystem where clients and artists can connect effortlessly, collaborate meaningfully, and bring ideas to life with confidence and clarity. We aim to break down barriers in the commission process by offering a platform that is transparent, user-friendly, and artist-centered. Flare is committed to supporting diverse creators, uplifting artistic expression, and making high-quality creative work accessible to everyone.

context

What
Flare’s target audience includes individuals seeking custom artwork as well as creators offering commission-based services. These users value a platform that simplifies discovering artists, posting requests, managing projects, and communicating clearly. They want a streamlined, trustworthy space where creative work can be commissioned efficiently, transparently, and without friction.

Who
The audience includes young adults and professionals (18–35), art enthusiasts, content creators, small business owners, and students who require custom visuals—such as illustrations, character designs, logos, or cover art. On the creator side, the audience consists of freelance artists, illustrators, animators, and designers looking for exposure, steady commission work, and reliable client communication.
Why
Flare empowers independent artists by giving them visibility, credibility, and a professional space to grow their craft and client base.
When
Users turn to Flare anytime creativity calls. Whether it’s a spontaneous idea, a late-night spark, or a planned project, they can browse artists, post requests, and start commissions whenever inspiration appears.
Where
Users access Flare from any connected space, switching smoothly between mobile and desktop as they create and collaborate.

  • Transparent commission process

Freelance Illustrator Working from Home Studio

Goals

  • Grow a stable client base
  • Receive clear project briefs
  • Showcase his work professionally

Frustrations

  • Clients changing scope or ghosting
  • Hard to track feedback across platforms
  • Disorganized commission flow

Needs

  • Tools that streamline delivery and communication
  • A platform that supports long-term growth
  • A structured workflow for quotes & revisions

With the user personas in mind, I planned out three user flows for this project:
1. Browse and purchase an artwork service from an aritst.
2. Browse the Showcase feed and interact with an artist.
3. Create an original character reference sheet for more convenient communication with artists.

Future Development

As my first application UI project, Flare gave me the opportunity to transform conceptual design skills into a complete, end-to-end product experience. I learned how user personas shape real design decisions and how user flows emerge directly from the needs and behaviors of different audiences. I also became more confident in Figma, especially in creating reusable components and iterating efficiently based on feedback. Overall, this experience helped me grow significantly as a designer and laid the foundation for how I approach planning, testing, and refining future UI/UX work.

In my future projects I want to improve on several points:
1. Refine navigation, clarity of information, mobile responsiveness, and onboarding flow.
2. Conduct user researches as well as testings to make sure the project is applicable
3. Add project management tools such as milestone tracking, version history, and file review.
